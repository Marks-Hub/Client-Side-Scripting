Pistachios nuts

Originating in parts of Western Asia and the Middle East, the pistachio (technically a seed) has proven its appeal as a key ingredient in dishes such as baklava, ma'amoul, haroseth, and halvah.

Hazelnuts

This round nut plays a big role in a beloved sweet spread gianduja, better known as the commercially available Nutella.

Cashews

This crescent-shaped South American nut made its way into other cuisines such as Indian and Thai, thanks to Portuguese explorers and traders.

Walnuts

The walnut is an extremely popular baking ingredient, and one of the few nuts you can still find in the shell (depending on the season).

Marcona Almonds

Rounder, flatter, and sweeter than regular almonds, Marcona almonds are from Spain.

Macadamia Nuts

Originally available only in Hawaii or Australia, these large and now ubiquitous nuts are palpably rich with an almost buttery flavor.
